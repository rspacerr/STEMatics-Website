Engineering: 3D Printing / TinkerCad

What is 3D Printing?

3D printing will be the engineering topic our classes will focuson during our first set of classes. 3D printing is
is defined as the process of three dimensional models of objects by creating the object in a digital application first.
For example, Autodesk Tinkercad is an application that can be used to construct the models digitally and will be the
platform we will be using in our lessons. Once the digital model is completed, the file is sent to a 3D printer which uses
a filament to create the physical model. Overall, 3D printing can be used to create interesting and creative models
both digitally and physically and the STEMatics club will teach students the basics of Tinkercad.

What is TinkerCad?

The engineering lessons will start by familiarizing students with 3D printing, and TinkerCad, a free-of-charge, online
3D modeling program. Our lessons will teach the basics of how to best use the tools featured in TinkerCad, and allow
students to apply these skills in an environment where help is accessible and they are free to ask questions. We also
offer video tutorials which students can choose to follow, or use for inspiration in their own 3D printing endeavors.
As of now, the main focus of the engineering section is to provide students with lessons regarding 3D printing, however,
we have future plans to expand into more engineering topics such as circuitry, laser cutting, and more.
